Now we will define our transition functions $\delta$:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $\delta(q_s,\epsilon,\bot)=(q,S\bot)$
    \item $S\to aSd$ is $\delta(q,a,S)=(q,Sd)$
    \item $S\to aTd$ is $\delta(q,a,S)=(q,Td)$
    \item $T\to{bbT}$ is split into $T\to{bV}$ and $V\to{bT}$
    \item $T\to{bV}$ is $\delta(q,b,T)=(q,V)$
    \item $V\to{bT}$ is $\delta(q,b,V)=(q,T)$
    \item $T\to{U}$ is $\delta(q,\epsilon,T)=(q,U)$
    \item $U\to{cU}$ is $\delta(q,c,U)=(q,U)$
    \item $U\to{c}$ is $\delta(q,c,U)=(q,\epsilon)$
    \item $\delta(q,\epsilon,\bot)=(q_f,\epsilon)$
\end{itemize}
For the NPDA, there are three states $q_s$, $q$, and $q_f$. This is because, without there being $q_s$, it would be possible for a string to be empty and pass the NPDA, which goes against the constrains of our language. Furthermore, matching the transitions of the CFG to those of the NPDA, the transitions are extremely intuitive. The stack reads the character, pushes the corresponding transition to the stack, and proceeds to the next character. Once the stack is complete (which coincides with completion of the string), The NPDA makes an $\epsilon$-transition to the accepting state.

The Turing machine works as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Mark $a$
    \item For marked $a$, move right until we reach $c$ or $Z$ (end of $b$/$Y$ substring).
    \item Move left until we reach our first $b$
    \item Once we reach $b$, mark it as $Y$
    \item Once we mark $Y$, move right until we reach $c$, and mark it $Z$
    \item Repeat steps 3-5 until we reach $a$ or $X$ (no more $b$ to mark)
    \item Move right, changing every $Y$ back to $b$
    \item Restart at step 1 with next available $a$
\end{enumerate}
If at any point we are not able to perform a step, it means that the string does not follow the rules of the language, and is subsequently rejected by the machine. Assume we have string $x=aabbcccc$. The steps work as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Mark $a$, then $Xabbcccc$
    \item Move right until $c$, then move left to $b$ and replace with $Y$. Then $XabYcccc$
    \item Move right until $c$ and replace. Then $XabYZccc$
    \item Move left until $b$ and replace. Then $XaYYZccc$
    \item Move right until $c$ and replace. Then $XaYYZZcc$
    \item Move left until $b$ and replace. We reach $a$ instead, so we move right and clear all $Y$. Then $XabbZZcc$
    \item Move left until we reach the starting character $\vdash$, then start back at step 1
    \item Once we are back at step 5 and we reach terminal character $\sqcup$ or $\dashv$, check that there are only $X$, $b$, and $Z$ 
    \item As this holds true, our final string is $XXbbZZZZ$ and the string is accepted
\end{enumerate}
